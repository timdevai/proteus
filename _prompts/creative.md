## prompt: Story Generator
**use-when**: writing a story, narrative, case study, or origin story
**template**:
Write a [GENRE] story about [CORE_PREMISE]. Protagonist: [PROTAGONIST]. Central conflict: [CONFLICT]. Tone: [TONE]. Length: [LENGTH]. The ending should feel [ENDING_FEEL]. Do not use clichés. Make the first sentence impossible to stop reading.

**variables**:
- GENRE: thriller / drama / sci-fi / personal essay / business case study / origin story
- CORE_PREMISE: the core idea or situation
- PROTAGONIST: who the story follows
- CONFLICT: the main tension or obstacle
- TONE: dark / hopeful / funny / matter-of-fact / urgent
- LENGTH: 200 words / 500 words / full short story
- ENDING_FEEL: satisfying / open / surprising / bittersweet

## prompt: Name Generator
**use-when**: naming a product, company, feature, project, or brand
**template**:
Generate 20 name options for [WHAT_YOU'RE_NAMING]. It should feel [DESIRED_FEELING]. Target audience: [AUDIENCE]. Avoid: [WHAT_TO_AVOID]. For each name: give the name, a one-sentence rationale, and flag any that might have trademark or meaning issues. Then pick your top 3 and explain why.

**variables**:
- WHAT_YOU'RE_NAMING: product, company, feature, project, etc.
- DESIRED_FEELING: e.g. technical and credible / playful and approachable / premium and minimal
- AUDIENCE: who will hear/read this name
- WHAT_TO_AVOID: names too similar to competitors, overused words, anything that dates it

## prompt: Tagline Writer
**use-when**: writing a tagline, slogan, or one-liner for a product or brand
**template**:
Write 10 taglines for [PRODUCT_OR_BRAND]. Core value: [CORE_VALUE]. Audience: [AUDIENCE]. Tone: [TONE]. Constraint: under 7 words each. Include: 3 benefit-led, 3 emotion-led, 2 contrarian, 2 ultra-minimal. Then pick your best one and explain what makes it work.

**variables**:
- PRODUCT_OR_BRAND: what you're writing for
- CORE_VALUE: the single most important thing it does or represents
- AUDIENCE: who this tagline needs to resonate with
- TONE: serious / playful / bold / understated

## prompt: Reframe
**use-when**: stuck on a problem — get a completely different perspective on it
**template**:
Reframe [PROBLEM_OR_SITUATION] from 4 different angles: (1) what does this look like if it's actually an opportunity, (2) what would someone from a completely different industry do with this, (3) what's the exact opposite of the obvious solution, (4) what would the problem look like if it were 10x worse — and why might that be better? Don't pick the "best" frame — just give me all four.

**variables**:
- PROBLEM_OR_SITUATION: describe what you're stuck on

## prompt: Dialogue Writer
**use-when**: writing a conversation between two people — for a script, story, sales scenario, or practice
**template**:
Write a [LENGTH] dialogue between [PERSON_A] and [PERSON_B] about [TOPIC]. [PERSON_A] wants [A_GOAL]. [PERSON_B] wants [B_GOAL]. The conversation should [DYNAMIC]. Make it realistic — people interrupt, misunderstand, and don't always say exactly what they mean.

**variables**:
- LENGTH: short (5-6 exchanges) / medium (10-15 exchanges) / long (full scene)
- PERSON_A: describe this character (role, personality, what they want)
- PERSON_B: describe this character
- TOPIC: what they're talking about
- A_GOAL: what Person A is trying to accomplish in this conversation
- B_GOAL: what Person B is trying to accomplish
- DYNAMIC: e.g. end in agreement / end in conflict / stay unresolved / one person convinces the other

## prompt: Scene Builder
**use-when**: writing a single scene — for a story, screenplay, game, or creative project
**template**:
Write a scene in [GENRE/STYLE] where [SETUP]. Location: [SETTING]. Characters present: [CHARACTERS]. What the scene must accomplish (plot function): [SCENE_GOAL]. Emotional undercurrent: [EMOTION]. Constraint: no exposition dumps. Show, don't tell. Make the last line land.

**variables**:
- GENRE/STYLE: literary fiction / noir thriller / dark comedy / YA / screenplay / game narrative
- SETUP: what happens in the scene (one sentence)
- SETTING: where and when — specific details, not generic
- CHARACTERS: who's there and what they want in this moment
- SCENE_GOAL: what must be true after this scene that wasn't true before
- EMOTION: the feeling that should run under the surface (dread / longing / tension / grief / relief)

## prompt: World Builder
**use-when**: creating a setting for a story, game, or creative project
**template**:
Build a world for [PROJECT_TYPE] set in [BASE_CONCEPT]. It should feel [DESIRED_FEEL]. Give me: the single rule that makes this world different from ours and all its downstream consequences, the power structure (who has it, who wants it, who lost it), the thing ordinary people argue about, and the secret that most people don't know but would change everything.

**variables**:
- PROJECT_TYPE: novel / game / short story / screenplay / tabletop RPG
- BASE_CONCEPT: the core premise (e.g. "near-future Earth where memory is tradeable" / "fantasy world where magic is dying")
- DESIRED_FEEL: gritty / hopeful / mysterious / oppressive / whimsical

## prompt: Character Builder
**use-when**: creating a character who feels like a real person, not a type
**template**:
Build a character for [PROJECT]. Role in the story: [STORY_ROLE]. Give them: a specific want (what they're chasing) and need (what they actually lack), a contradiction (the gap between how they see themselves and how others see them), a specific habit or mannerism, the moment that broke them (backstory they carry), and the thing they'd never admit. Then: what's their arc — who are they at the start vs. the end?

**variables**:
- PROJECT: what you're writing
- STORY_ROLE: protagonist / antagonist / mentor / love interest / comic relief / supporting

## prompt: Logline Generator
**use-when**: pitching a story — one sentence that makes someone want to read/watch it
**template**:
Generate 10 loglines for [STORY_CONCEPT]. Each under 30 words. Use the structure: [protagonist] must [goal] before/after [stakes], but [obstacle/twist]. Include: 3 variations that lead with character, 3 that lead with concept, 2 that lead with conflict, 2 that are deliberately counterintuitive. Pick your best one and explain what makes it work.

**variables**:
- STORY_CONCEPT: your story in 2-3 sentences

## prompt: Brand Voice Guide
**use-when**: defining how a brand sounds so anyone writing for it can stay consistent
**template**:
Create a brand voice guide for [BRAND]. The brand is: [BRAND_DESCRIPTION]. Target audience: [AUDIENCE]. Personality in 3 words: [PERSONALITY]. Give me: 5 things this brand always does (sentence structure, word choices, tone), 5 things it never does, 3 before/after rewrites showing the voice in action, and a one-paragraph "voice brief" a copywriter can use as a quick reference.

**variables**:
- BRAND: brand name and what it does
- BRAND_DESCRIPTION: what the company does and what it stands for
- AUDIENCE: who reads/hears this brand
- PERSONALITY: 3 adjectives (e.g. "direct, irreverent, smart")

## prompt: Villain Designer
**use-when**: creating an antagonist who feels motivated and dangerous, not just evil
**template**:
Design a villain for [STORY]. The protagonist's goal: [HERO_GOAL]. The villain's goal: [VILLAIN_GOAL]. What they have in common: [SHARED_QUALITY]. Give them: a motivation the reader can understand even if they disagree with it, a method that reveals their worldview, a moment of genuine humanity, and why they believe they're right. Make them the hero of their own story.

**variables**:
- STORY: brief description of your story
- HERO_GOAL: what the protagonist wants
- VILLAIN_GOAL: what the antagonist wants (make it genuinely opposed, not just "take over the world")
- SHARED_QUALITY: something they both value or have in common (creates real tension)

## prompt: Pitch Generator
**use-when**: pitching a creative project — to a publisher, producer, investor, or collaborator
**template**:
Write a pitch for [PROJECT_TYPE]: [PROJECT_DESCRIPTION]. Audience for the pitch: [WHO_YOU'RE_PITCHING]. Length: [LENGTH]. Include: a hook in the first sentence, what makes it different from what's already out there, who the audience is and why they'll care, and a clear ask. No passive voice. No hedging.

**variables**:
- PROJECT_TYPE: novel / podcast / film / game / series / newsletter / course
- PROJECT_DESCRIPTION: describe your project honestly and specifically
- WHO_YOU'RE_PITCHING: agent / publisher / investor / collaborator / audience
- LENGTH: one paragraph / half-page / full page

## prompt: Twist Generator
**use-when**: your story's ending or midpoint is predictable — find the version that surprises
**template**:
My [STORY_ELEMENT] is [CURRENT_VERSION]. Generate 8 alternative versions, in order from "slightly subverted" to "completely unexpected." For each: what assumption it breaks, whether it serves the theme, and whether the setup supports it. Then: which one is the most satisfying twist and what would need to change in the setup to earn it?

**variables**:
- STORY_ELEMENT: ending / plot twist / character revelation / scene outcome
- CURRENT_VERSION: describe what you currently have

## prompt: Metaphor Miner
**use-when**: abstract concepts need concrete language — for speeches, writing, or presentations
**template**:
Find [NUMBER] original metaphors for [ABSTRACT_CONCEPT]. Context: explaining it to [AUDIENCE]. Avoid: [OVERUSED_METAPHORS]. For each metaphor: state it, extend it (push the comparison as far as it holds), and flag where it breaks down. Then pick the best two for completely different audiences.

**variables**:
- NUMBER: 5 / 10 / 15
- ABSTRACT_CONCEPT: what you're trying to make concrete (e.g. "compound interest," "bureaucracy," "machine learning," "grief")
- AUDIENCE: who you're explaining it to
- OVERUSED_METAPHORS: the tired comparisons everyone uses for this concept

## prompt: Satire Writer
**use-when**: writing something funny and pointed — a piece that makes a real argument through humor
**template**:
Write a [FORMAT] satirizing [TARGET]. Point of view: [PERSONA]. Real argument underneath: [ACTUAL_ARGUMENT]. Style: [STYLE]. Rules: commit to the bit completely, don't break character to explain the joke, and make sure someone who disagrees with the argument still finds it funny.

**variables**:
- FORMAT: op-ed / press release / memo / listicle / product announcement / FAQ
- TARGET: the institution, trend, behavior, or idea being mocked
- PERSONA: who's "writing" this (e.g. a clueless PR team / an AI press release / a concerned consultant)
- ACTUAL_ARGUMENT: what you're actually arguing (the real point under the joke)
- STYLE: dry / absurdist / deadpan / savage
