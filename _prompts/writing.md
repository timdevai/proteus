## prompt: Email Writer
**use-when**: writing any professional email — cold outreach, follow-up, request, response
**template**:
Write a [TONE] email from [SENDER_CONTEXT] to [RECIPIENT_ROLE] about [PURPOSE]. Goal: [DESIRED_OUTCOME]. Length: under [LENGTH] words. No fluff. End with one clear ask. Subject line included.

**variables**:
- TONE: professional / friendly / direct / urgent
- SENDER_CONTEXT: who you are and why you're relevant (1 sentence)
- RECIPIENT_ROLE: their role or relationship to you
- PURPOSE: what the email is about
- DESIRED_OUTCOME: what you want them to do
- LENGTH: 100 / 150 / 200

## prompt: Editor
**use-when**: improving a draft — tightening, fixing flow, removing filler
**template**:
Edit this [CONTENT_TYPE] for [GOAL]. Cut every word that doesn't earn its place. Fix flow. Keep the author's voice. Don't add fluff. Show the rewrite, then a 3-bullet summary of what changed and why.

Content to edit:
[PASTE_CONTENT]

**variables**:
- CONTENT_TYPE: email / essay / report / post / script / bio
- GOAL: clarity / persuasion / brevity / tone-shift / formality
- PASTE_CONTENT: paste your draft

## prompt: Explainer
**use-when**: explaining a complex topic simply — for a presentation, email to a non-expert, or your own understanding
**template**:
Explain [TOPIC] to [AUDIENCE] who [AUDIENCE_CONTEXT]. Use an analogy. No jargon. Under [LENGTH] words. End with the 1 thing they need to remember.

**variables**:
- TOPIC: what you're explaining
- AUDIENCE: who you're explaining it to (e.g. a 15-year-old, a non-technical manager, a new client)
- AUDIENCE_CONTEXT: what they already know / don't know
- LENGTH: 150 / 300 / 500

## prompt: Rewrite in Style
**use-when**: rewriting content to match a specific voice, format, or style
**template**:
Rewrite the following in the style of [STYLE_REFERENCE]. Match: sentence length, vocabulary level, structure, and tone. Keep all the original ideas. Do not add new information.

Original:
[PASTE_CONTENT]

Style to match: [STYLE_REFERENCE]

**variables**:
- STYLE_REFERENCE: Paul Graham essays / Ernest Hemingway / a legal brief / a tweet thread / a McKinsey slide / casual text message
- PASTE_CONTENT: your original text

## prompt: Summary
**use-when**: summarizing a long document, article, transcript, or meeting
**template**:
Summarize [CONTENT_TYPE] in [FORMAT]. Audience: [AUDIENCE]. Focus on: [FOCUS]. Skip: [WHAT_TO_OMIT]. End with 3 action items if any exist.

[PASTE_CONTENT]

**variables**:
- CONTENT_TYPE: article / meeting transcript / report / contract / research paper
- FORMAT: 3 bullet points / 1 paragraph / executive summary with headers
- AUDIENCE: who will read this (you / your boss / a client / general public)
- FOCUS: decisions made / key data / recommendations / risks
- WHAT_TO_OMIT: background / methodology / examples (whatever is low priority)
- PASTE_CONTENT: paste the content to summarize

## prompt: Voice Cloner
**use-when**: writing content that needs to sound exactly like a specific person or brand
**template**:
Study these samples of [PERSON/BRAND]'s writing:

[SAMPLE_1]

[SAMPLE_2]

Now write [NEW_CONTENT_REQUEST] in their exact voice. Match: sentence rhythm, word choice, how they open paragraphs, how they end them, what they never say.

**variables**:
- PERSON/BRAND: whose voice you're cloning
- SAMPLE_1, SAMPLE_2: paste 2-3 real writing samples from them
- NEW_CONTENT_REQUEST: what you want written in their voice

## prompt: Thread Writer
**use-when**: turning an idea or article into a tweet/X thread that gets shared
**template**:
Turn the following into a [LENGTH]-tweet thread on [TOPIC]. Hook: make the first tweet impossible to scroll past. Rules: each tweet stands alone, no tweet ends with "..." as a cliffhanger, last tweet has one clear call to action. No filler. No "in conclusion."

Source material:
[PASTE_CONTENT]

**variables**:
- LENGTH: 5 / 8 / 12 tweets
- TOPIC: the core idea or angle
- PASTE_CONTENT: paste the article, idea, or notes

## prompt: Newsletter Intro
**use-when**: writing the opening hook for a newsletter issue
**template**:
Write the opening section (under 100 words) for a newsletter issue about [TOPIC]. The reader skims the first 2 sentences to decide if they read on. Hook them with something surprising, contrarian, or personal — not "this week we cover." Tone: [TONE]. End with a one-sentence bridge to the main content.

**variables**:
- TOPIC: what this issue covers
- TONE: conversational / intellectual / punchy / warm

## prompt: Landing Page Copy
**use-when**: writing a landing page from scratch or rewriting weak copy
**template**:
Write landing page copy for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Visitor: [WHO_LANDS_HERE]. Their #1 problem: [PROBLEM]. Our solution in one sentence: [SOLUTION]. Social proof available: [PROOF]. Include: hero headline, subheadline, 3 benefits (outcome-focused, not feature-focused), one objection crusher, and CTA. No corporate speak.

**variables**:
- PRODUCT/SERVICE: what you're selling
- WHO_LANDS_HERE: who visits this page and why
- PROBLEM: the pain they feel before finding you
- SOLUTION: what you do in plain English
- PROOF: testimonials, metrics, client names you can use

## prompt: Bio Writer
**use-when**: writing a professional bio for a website, LinkedIn, conference, or pitch deck
**template**:
Write a [LENGTH] professional bio for [PERSON]. Their role: [ROLE]. Key credentials: [CREDENTIALS]. What makes them different from others in this field: [DIFFERENTIATOR]. Audience: [AUDIENCE]. Write in [PERSPECTIVE] person. No filler phrases like "passionate about" or "dedicated to."

**variables**:
- LENGTH: one sentence / 3 sentences / one paragraph / full page
- PERSON: name and context
- ROLE: current title and what they actually do
- CREDENTIALS: relevant experience, results, or background
- DIFFERENTIATOR: what makes them stand out
- AUDIENCE: who reads this (investors / conference attendees / potential clients / general public)
- PERSPECTIVE: first / third

## prompt: Objection Handler
**use-when**: responding to pushback on a proposal, price, idea, or recommendation
**template**:
Someone just said: "[OBJECTION]". My position: [MY_POSITION]. Write a response that: acknowledges their concern without being defensive, reframes the objection into a reason to proceed, and ends with a question that keeps the conversation moving. Under 100 words. Don't say "I understand your concern."

**variables**:
- OBJECTION: the exact pushback you received
- MY_POSITION: what you're trying to convince them of

## prompt: Press Release
**use-when**: announcing a product launch, partnership, funding, or milestone
**template**:
Write a press release for [ANNOUNCEMENT]. Company: [COMPANY]. Date: [DATE]. Key facts: [FACTS]. Quote from [SPOKESPERSON]: [QUOTE_CONTEXT] (write the quote — make it sound like a real person, not a PR robot). Format: standard AP style. Lead with the news, not the backstory.

**variables**:
- ANNOUNCEMENT: what happened
- COMPANY: company name and one-line description
- DATE: release date (or "FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE")
- FACTS: the 3-5 most important facts about the announcement
- SPOKESPERSON: who's quoted (name and title)
- QUOTE_CONTEXT: what the quote should convey

## prompt: FAQ Generator
**use-when**: building a FAQ section, anticipating customer questions, or training support staff
**template**:
Generate [NUMBER] FAQs for [PRODUCT/SERVICE/TOPIC]. Include: the questions customers actually ask (not the ones you wish they asked), questions that reveal confusion about pricing or scope, and 2 questions people should ask but don't. Answer each one in under 50 words. Conversational tone.

**variables**:
- NUMBER: 10 / 15 / 20
- PRODUCT/SERVICE/TOPIC: what the FAQ covers

## prompt: Persuasion Framework
**use-when**: structuring an argument, pitch, or request to maximize the chance of a yes
**template**:
Help me structure a persuasive [FORMAT] for [GOAL]. Audience: [AUDIENCE]. What they care about: [THEIR_PRIORITIES]. What I'm asking for: [THE_ASK]. Constraints: [CONSTRAINTS]. Use a proven framework (AIDA / Problem-Solution / Before-After-Bridge / etc.) and tell me which one and why. Write the full piece.

**variables**:
- FORMAT: email / pitch deck outline / conversation script / memo / proposal
- GOAL: what you want to persuade them of
- AUDIENCE: who you're persuading (their role, mindset, objections)
- THEIR_PRIORITIES: what they optimize for (cost, risk, time, status)
- THE_ASK: exactly what you want from them
- CONSTRAINTS: word count, tone, formality level

## prompt: Job Description Writer
**use-when**: hiring — writing a job post that attracts the right candidates and repels wrong ones
**template**:
Write a job description for a [ROLE] at [COMPANY_TYPE]. What they'll actually do day-to-day: [REAL_WORK]. Must-haves: [REQUIREMENTS]. What makes this role compelling vs. others: [UPSIDE]. Red flags we want to filter out: [FILTER]. Keep it under 400 words. No corporate mission statements.

**variables**:
- ROLE: the job title
- COMPANY_TYPE: startup / agency / enterprise / remote-first / etc.
- REAL_WORK: the actual tasks, not vague "drive growth" language
- REQUIREMENTS: the 3-5 non-negotiable qualifications
- UPSIDE: what a great candidate would be excited about
- FILTER: the type of candidate you don't want (e.g. "people who need heavy process" or "people who hate ambiguity")
