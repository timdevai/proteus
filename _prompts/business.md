## prompt: Positioning Statement
**use-when**: figuring out how to describe what you do and who it's for
**template**:
Write a positioning statement for [PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE]. Target customer: [TARGET]. Their problem: [PROBLEM]. My solution: [SOLUTION]. What makes it different: [DIFFERENTIATOR]. Format: one sentence for a homepage hero, one sentence for a cold email opener, one sentence for a verbal pitch.

**variables**:
- PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE: what you're selling
- TARGET: who the ideal customer is (be specific — not "small businesses", but "solo consultants who charge by the hour")
- PROBLEM: the specific pain they have
- SOLUTION: what you actually do
- DIFFERENTIATOR: why yours over alternatives

## prompt: Pricing Calculator
**use-when**: figuring out what to charge — productized service, retainer, or one-time project
**template**:
Help me price [OFFER]. My costs: [COSTS]. Target margin: [MARGIN]%. Comparable alternatives charge: [MARKET_RATE]. My differentiation: [DIFFERENTIATOR]. My target customer's budget range: [BUDGET]. Give me: recommended price, the psychology behind it, whether to anchor high or low, and a one-line justification I can use with clients who push back.

**variables**:
- OFFER: what you're pricing (e.g. monthly retainer, project, digital product)
- COSTS: your time, tools, and delivery costs
- MARGIN: target profit margin %
- MARKET_RATE: what alternatives cost
- DIFFERENTIATOR: why yours is worth more (or less)
- BUDGET: what your target customer typically spends on this kind of thing

## prompt: Cold Sales Email
**use-when**: writing a B2B cold email that actually gets replies
**template**:
Write a cold sales email from [SENDER] to [TARGET_ROLE] at [TARGET_COMPANY_TYPE]. My offer: [OFFER]. Their likely problem: [PROBLEM]. Social proof I can use: [PROOF]. Length: under 100 words. One specific ask at the end. No "I hope this email finds you well." No feature dump. Lead with their problem, not my product.

**variables**:
- SENDER: your name and company/role
- TARGET_ROLE: job title of the person you're emailing
- TARGET_COMPANY_TYPE: type of company (e.g. Series A SaaS startup, mid-size law firm)
- OFFER: what you're selling in one sentence
- PROBLEM: the specific problem they have that you solve
- PROOF: one concrete result or client name you can reference

## prompt: Competitor Analysis
**use-when**: understanding the market before launching, pitching, or repositioning
**template**:
Analyze the competitive landscape for [PRODUCT_CATEGORY]. I'm building [MY_PRODUCT]. List the top 5 competitors, their positioning, pricing, main strengths, and the gap they leave open. Then tell me: where is there a defensible position that isn't already occupied, and what would I need to do to own it?

**variables**:
- PRODUCT_CATEGORY: the market you're entering
- MY_PRODUCT: what you're building and for whom

## prompt: Launch Checklist
**use-when**: before launching anything — product, campaign, service, content piece
**template**:
Create a pre-launch checklist for [WHAT_I'M_LAUNCHING]. Launch date: [DATE]. Audience: [AUDIENCE]. Distribution channel: [CHANNEL]. Check: what must be true on day 1, what can be fixed in week 1, what I'm probably forgetting, and the single most common launch failure mode for this type of thing.

**variables**:
- WHAT_I'M_LAUNCHING: product, service, campaign, content, etc.
- DATE: when you're launching
- AUDIENCE: who will see/use it
- CHANNEL: how people will discover it (Gumroad, Twitter, email list, direct sales, etc.)

## prompt: Value Proposition Designer
**use-when**: your offering isn't connecting with customers — something in the message is off
**template**:
I sell [PRODUCT/SERVICE] to [CUSTOMER]. They keep [CUSTOMER_BEHAVIOR] instead of buying or staying. Help me redesign the value proposition. Identify: the job they're really hiring this for (not what I think), the anxiety blocking the purchase, and the gain they want that I'm not leading with. Rewrite my pitch using those three inputs.

**variables**:
- PRODUCT/SERVICE: what you sell
- CUSTOMER: who buys (or should buy)
- CUSTOMER_BEHAVIOR: what they do instead (buy from competitor, do it themselves, do nothing)

## prompt: Unit Economics Builder
**use-when**: validating that a business model actually makes money at scale
**template**:
Build the unit economics for [BUSINESS_MODEL]. Inputs: [KEY_METRICS]. Calculate: CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, payback period, and contribution margin per unit. Tell me: at what scale does this become healthy, what's the single metric that most needs to improve, and what's the fastest lever to pull?

**variables**:
- BUSINESS_MODEL: e.g. monthly SaaS, one-time product, service retainer, marketplace
- KEY_METRICS: whatever you know — price, churn rate, cost to acquire, delivery cost, avg order value

## prompt: Customer Avatar
**use-when**: you're guessing at who your customer is instead of knowing precisely
**template**:
Build a customer avatar for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Based on what I know: [WHAT_I_KNOW_ABOUT_CUSTOMERS]. Fill in: their demographic reality, a day in their life before and after using this, their biggest fear related to this problem, the words they use to describe the problem (not marketing language), where they spend time online, and what they read/watch. End with: the one insight that should change my messaging.

**variables**:
- PRODUCT/SERVICE: what you sell
- WHAT_I_KNOW_ABOUT_CUSTOMERS: any data, interviews, or observations you have

## prompt: Investor Pitch Critic
**use-when**: preparing a pitch deck or investor narrative — find the holes before they do
**template**:
I'm pitching [COMPANY/PROJECT] to [INVESTOR_TYPE]. Here's my narrative: [PITCH_SUMMARY]. Attack it from an investor's perspective: what question will they definitely ask that I haven't answered, what will make them pass in the first 2 minutes, what number will they push back on, and what's the one thing I need to nail to get a second meeting?

**variables**:
- COMPANY/PROJECT: what you're pitching
- INVESTOR_TYPE: seed VC / angel / accelerator / strategic / bank
- PITCH_SUMMARY: describe your current narrative (or paste the deck outline)

## prompt: Churn Analyzer
**use-when**: customers are leaving and you need to understand why before you can fix it
**template**:
Help me diagnose churn for [PRODUCT]. Known data: [WHAT_I_KNOW]. Segment the likely reasons into: expected churn (solved the job, moved on), preventable churn (something broke their experience), and competitive churn (they found something better). For the preventable category: what are the top 3 causes, what signals predicted it, and what intervention would have worked?

**variables**:
- PRODUCT: what you sell
- WHAT_I_KNOW: churn rate, when customers cancel, any exit survey data, usage patterns

## prompt: Partnership Pitch
**use-when**: approaching a potential partner — integration, co-marketing, distribution, or referral
**template**:
Write a partnership pitch from [MY_COMPANY] to [PARTNER_COMPANY]. What we offer: [MY_VALUE]. What I need from them: [WHAT_I_WANT]. Why this is worth their time: [THEIR_WIN]. Format: under 200 words, no fluff, lead with their upside not mine, specific ask at the end. Make it sound like it was written by a founder, not a business development template.

**variables**:
- MY_COMPANY: your company and what it does in one sentence
- PARTNER_COMPANY: who you're approaching and what they do
- MY_VALUE: what you bring to the partnership
- WHAT_I_WANT: distribution / integration / co-marketing / data / access / referrals
- THEIR_WIN: why this is worth a yes for them

## prompt: Pricing Psychology
**use-when**: setting or adjusting pricing for maximum conversion, not just margin
**template**:
Help me price [OFFER]. Current price: [CURRENT_PRICE]. Conversion rate: [CONVERSION]. My goal: [GOAL]. Apply pricing psychology: anchoring, decoy pricing, charm pricing, payment frequency framing, and bundling. Recommend the best structure given my goal. Explain the psychology behind your recommendation and what I'd need to test first.

**variables**:
- OFFER: what you're selling
- CURRENT_PRICE: what you charge now (or "haven't launched yet")
- CONVERSION: if you know it (or "unknown")
- GOAL: increase revenue / increase volume / increase perceived value / reduce churn

## prompt: Onboarding Flow Designer
**use-when**: users sign up but don't activate — design a flow that gets them to first value fast
**template**:
Design an onboarding flow for [PRODUCT]. The "aha moment" — when users first get real value: [AHA_MOMENT]. Current time-to-aha: [CURRENT_TIME]. Where users drop off: [DROP_OFF_POINT]. Give me: the shortest possible path from signup to aha, what to remove from the current flow, what to add, and what the first email/message should say (and when to send it).

**variables**:
- PRODUCT: what you're building
- AHA_MOMENT: the exact action or outcome that makes users "get it"
- CURRENT_TIME: how long it takes new users to reach the aha moment (hours / days / never)
- DROP_OFF_POINT: where users abandon the flow

## prompt: Objection Map
**use-when**: sales calls stalling, emails not getting replies — map what's really in the way
**template**:
Map the objections blocking sales for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Price is [PRICE_POINT]. Typical customer: [CUSTOMER]. List the top 5 objections in order of frequency, the real concern behind each (not the stated one), the best response for each, and the objection that signals "not a fit" vs. "needs more information." Format as a quick-reference table.

**variables**:
- PRODUCT/SERVICE: what you sell
- PRICE_POINT: what you charge
- CUSTOMER: describe your typical buyer

## prompt: Referral System Designer
**use-when**: growing through word of mouth — make the ask systematic, not accidental
**template**:
Design a referral system for [BUSINESS]. Current customer type: [CUSTOMER]. What they'd share: [SHAREABLE_OUTCOME]. My constraint: [CONSTRAINT]. Give me: the trigger (when to ask), the ask (exact words), the incentive (what makes them say yes), the frictionless share mechanism, and what to do when someone gets referred. Keep it simple enough to run manually first.

**variables**:
- BUSINESS: what you do
- CUSTOMER: who your customers are
- SHAREABLE_OUTCOME: the result that makes them want to tell people (the story they'd tell)
- CONSTRAINT: no engineering budget / no discount headroom / B2B only / etc.
